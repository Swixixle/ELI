# CFO Value Model: ELI Imaging Ontology Audit

**Audit Date:** January 2026  
**Status:** Recommendation (Not Deployed)  
**Purpose:** Re-evaluate CFO value model assumptions under ELI Imaging positioning

---

## 1. Executive Summary

This audit re-examines the CFO value model under the ELI Imaging ontology. The original model was calibrated for "AI governance" positioning, which carried skepticism discounts. Under the imaging framing, certain conservative assumptions can be relaxed while maintaining governance credibility.

**Key Finding:** The model structure remains sound. Credibility caps may be adjusted upward based on imaging discipline, but the core logic (no causality claims, uncertainty reduction only) is preserved.

---

## 2. Assumptions Review

### 2.1 Overly Conservative Under Imaging

| Assumption | Original Framing | Issue Under Imaging |
|------------|------------------|---------------------|
| **Escalation Avoidance Cap (12–15%)** | Conservative due to AI causality fears | Imaging produces verifiable procedural visibility, not predictions. CFOs can book informational value without requiring outcome proof. |
| **Duration Savings Cap (7.5–10%)** | Assumed uncertainty about process acceleration | Imaging discipline creates audit-verifiable checkpoints. Duration reduction is measurable, not inferred. |
| **Value Attribution Language** | Avoided any claim of "prevented" or "improved" | Imaging terminology ("resolved opacity", "visibility yield") is defensible without causal claims. |

### 2.2 Must Remain Conservative

| Assumption | Why It Must Stay |
|------------|-----------------|
| **No outcome causality claims** | Imaging does not diagnose; it determines whether diagnosis is possible. Any claim of "prevented errors" violates the imaging model. |
| **Break-even anchoring** | Pilot pricing must remain below break-even to preserve pilot optionality and avoid over-promise. |
| **Credibility cap structure** | Caps are mandatory governance protection. They may be recalibrated, not eliminated. |
| **Gross Risk Surface calculation** | The surface calculation reflects existing exposure, not ELI-created value. This is measurement, not intervention. |

---

## 3. Variable Re-Interpretation (Imaging Alignment)

The following variables are re-labeled for imaging consistency. **Math stays the same.**

| Original Variable | Imaging Re-Interpretation |
|-------------------|---------------------------|
| Escalation rates | Probability of procedural opacity reaching authority |
| Duration | Ambiguity window length (time between decision and resolution) |
| Avoidance | Opacity resolved earlier through imaging scan |
| Risk surface | Gross procedural uncertainty exposure |
| Value realized | Uncertainty made visible (not outcomes changed) |

**Confirmation:** All variable descriptions now reflect **resolution**, not **intervention**.

| Cap | Original Range | Imaging-Adjusted Range | Justification |

**Note:** These are not guarantees. They represent the upper bound of defensible informational value under imaging discipline.

## 5. New Derived Metric: Procedural Resolution Yield (PRY)

### Definition

```
PRY = (Adjusted Avoided + Adjusted Duration) / Gross Risk Surface
```

### Interpretation

PRY is **not ROI**. It is an imaging yield ratio that expresses:

> "What fraction of the gross procedural uncertainty becomes visible through imaging?"

| PRY Range | Interpretation |
|-----------|----------------|
| < 15% | Low imaging yield — limited procedural visibility |
| 15–25% | Moderate imaging yield — standard governance benefit |
| 25–35% | Strong imaging yield — high procedural resolution |
| > 35% | Exceptional — review for optimism bias |

**Usage:** Executive interpretation only. Not for pricing or contractual claims.

| Parameter | Value |

**Flag:** This creates **pricing optionality** — not guaranteed value. We do NOT auto-raise prices without human decision.

## 8. Recommendations

1. **Adopt imaging-adjusted caps** (Cap A: 15–20%, Cap B: 10–15%) for internal modeling and enterprise sales conversations.

2. **Introduce PRY metric** as an executive interpretation tool — not for pricing or guarantees.

3. **Maintain pilot pricing below break-even** to preserve optionality and avoid over-promise.

4. **Re-label all model variables** in any future documentation to use imaging terminology (opacity, visibility, resolution) rather than intervention language (prevented, improved, avoided).

5. **Do not implement UI changes** until this audit is reviewed and approved.

---

## 9. Closing Statement

This is a **maturity adjustment**, not a hype adjustment.

The original model was conservative because the system was still framed as "AI governance." Under ELI Imaging:

- Refusals are safety features
- Outcome-blindness is a strength
- Audit verifiability is central

CFOs are now allowed to book more informational value without demanding causal proof — because imaging produces **visibility**, not **predictions**.
