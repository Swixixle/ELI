# ELI Imaging — 5-Minute Demo Script

**A narrative that doesn't oversell**

---

## Setup (30 seconds)

> "I'm going to show you a governance system that does something unusual: it refuses to make decisions.
>
> Instead, it answers one question: **Is this case procedurally ready for review?**
>
> That's it. No predictions. No blame. No outcomes. Just: are we allowed to evaluate this?"

---

## Demo Flow

### Scene 1: The Empty Case (60 seconds)

**Action:** Open a new case with no documents or decision target.

> "Right now, this case has nothing. No decision target, no documents, no timeline.
>
> Watch what happens when I ask the system to evaluate it."

**Action:** Click evaluate or show the procedural checklist.

> "Zero out of five prerequisites met. The system says: **Advisory Only**.
>
> That means: you can look at this case, but you cannot issue a defensible judgment. The structural basis isn't there."

---

### Scene 2: Adding Structure (90 seconds)

**Action:** Set a decision target.

> "Now I'll tell the system what decision we're trying to support."

**Action:** Add 2-3 documents with dates.

> "These documents establish a timeline. The system can now verify temporal sequence."

**Action:** Re-evaluate.

> "We're now at 3 out of 5 prerequisites. The status changes to: **Review Permitted — High Procedural Risk**.
>
> That's a real posture. It means: you can proceed, but there are gaps. The system tells you exactly what's missing."

---

### Scene 3: The Immutable Record (60 seconds)

**Action:** Navigate to printouts, issue a judgment printout.

> "Once we're satisfied with the procedural posture, we can issue a printout.
>
> This creates a permanent record: cryptographically signed, timestamped, and immutable.
>
> Watch — if I try to delete or modify it..."

**Action:** Show 403 rejection (or explain it).

> "The system refuses. This record now exists forever. It's the answer to: 'What did we know, and when did we know it?'"

---

### Scene 4: What It Refuses To Do (60 seconds)

> "Here's what ELI will never do:
>
> - It won't tell you who was at fault.
> - It won't evaluate whether the outcome was correct.
> - It won't use hindsight to judge past decisions.
>
> If you ask 'Why did this fail?' after setting a past decision time, the system will refuse.
>
> That's not a bug. That's the point.
>
> Hindsight is inadmissible evidence."

---

### Scene 5: The Value Proposition (30 seconds)

> "So what do you actually get?
>
> 1. **Consistency** — Same prerequisites, every time.
> 2. **Defensibility** — Signed, immutable records.
> 3. **Restraint** — A system that knows what it's not allowed to say.
>
> ELI doesn't make decisions. It makes sure you're allowed to make them."

---

## Closing (30 seconds)

> "This is infrastructure for decisions that have to withstand scrutiny.
>
> If you're dealing with audits, regulators, or litigation — this is how you answer:
>
> 'The case wasn't ready then. Here's what changed.'
>
> Questions?"

---

## Key Phrases to Use

- "Procedural admissibility, not substantive judgment"
- "Jurisdiction before judgment"
- "The system refuses — that's the feature"
- "Immutable record"
- "Hindsight is inadmissible"

## Key Phrases to Avoid

- "AI decision-maker"
- "Compliance oracle"
- "Ethics engine"
- "The AI determined..."
- "Accuracy" (use "invariant satisfaction" instead)

---

## Demo Checklist

Before the demo:
- [ ] Create a fresh case (or use a prepared one)
- [ ] Have 2-3 sample documents ready to upload
- [ ] Know the decision target you'll set
- [ ] Have the printout route bookmarked

During the demo:
- [ ] Start with zero prerequisites (show the empty state)
- [ ] Build up to 3/5 (show the progression)
- [ ] Issue a printout (show immutability)
- [ ] Mention what ELI refuses to do

After the demo:
- [ ] Offer to show the About page for deeper explanation
- [ ] Offer access to the system for exploration
