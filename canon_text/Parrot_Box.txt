Abstract

This thesis introduces and formalizes The Parrot Box, an epistemic constraint model designed
to prevent false inference, retrospective moralization, and performative certainty in systems of
judgment. The Parrot Box defines a principled boundary between what may be known, what
may be inferred, and what must remain unsaid. It is not a theory of truth production, but a theory
of truth refusal‚ÄĒa mechanism that preserves epistemic integrity by constraining speech acts
when conditions for legitimate knowledge are absent.

The Parrot Box is positioned as a response to endemic failures in contemporary evaluative
systems: hindsight bias, outcome substitution, proxy certainty, and moral theater. Drawing on
philosophy of knowledge, decision theory, jurisprudence, and systems engineering, this work
argues that many institutional harms arise not from ignorance, but from illegitimate speech
under conditions of uncertainty. The Parrot Box provides a formal structure for silence,
delimitation, and refusal as positive epistemic acts.
