
ELI Expert — System Readout (January 2026)
What It Is
ELI Expert is a procedural admissibility gate — a governance system that determines whether case reviews are procedurally permitted before substantive evaluation begins.

"ELI doesn't make decisions. It makes sure we're allowed to make them."
Core Positioning
What ELI does: Evaluates whether case reviews are procedurally permitted.

What ELI does NOT do: Evaluate outcomes, clinical correctness, or fault. Those require human expertise. ELI determines jurisdiction, not judgment.

The 5 Procedural Prerequisites (Canon v4.0)
Before any case can be evaluated, it must satisfy:

Decision Target — What decision is this case trying to support?
Temporal Verification — When was the decision made? Can we verify the timeline?
Independent Verification — Is there third-party confirmation of claims?
Policy Application — What rule, policy, or standard governed this decision?
Contextual Constraints — What constraints shaped the decision-maker's options?
Threshold Policy
Prerequisites Met	Status
0-2	Advisory only — review unsafe
3	Review permitted — high procedural risk
4	Review strong — defensible
5	Review robust — regulator-ready
Two-Stage Workflow
Evaluation (/evaluate) — Checks procedural readiness. Reversible. You can add more evidence and re-evaluate.

Determination (/determine) — Creates a cryptographically signed receipt. Irreversible. The signed posture becomes part of the permanent record.

Immutable Judgment Printouts
Once a determination is made, users can issue a judgment printout — an immutable, cryptographically signed record that:

Cannot be modified (403 rejection on mutation attempts)
Cannot be deleted
Includes SHA-256 content hash and Ed25519 signature
Answers: "What did we know, and when did we know it?"
Operating Modes
Builder Mode — Active case construction. Upload documents, set decision targets, build timeline evidence. Focuses on gathering the procedural prerequisites.

Audit Mode — Review and verification. Evaluate procedural readiness, issue determinations, and generate immutable judgment records. Read-only posture.

What Makes This Provable
ELI's value is provable through invariant satisfaction, not accuracy claims:

Presence/absence of required inputs (binary, testable)
Ordering of events (deterministic)
Consistency between artifacts (hashable)
Immutability after issuance (cryptographically enforced)
A regulator, auditor, or engineer can verify:

"Was the decision time set?" → Yes/No
"Was the policy effective at that time?" → Yes/No
"Has the record been altered?" → Cryptographic proof
Technical Foundation
Capability	Implementation
Case state integrity	SHA-256 hashing
Non-repudiation	Ed25519 signing
Immutability	403 rejection on mutation
Replayability	Deterministic serialization
Key Behavioral Constraints
Outcome-blindness enforced: Cannot use knowledge of outcomes to evaluate decisions made before those outcomes occurred
Hindsight is inadmissible: System refuses temporal boundary violations
Category errors redirected: Questions requiring legal/moral judgment are redirected to appropriate human expertise
Refusal is a valid answer: When epistemic entitlement is absent, the system refuses rather than guesses
