The correct mental model: Decision Readiness Equation

Every decision type has a finite set of required evidentiary components.

You should treat it like a gate, not an opinion.

Example (conceptual)

Decision Readiness =
  Temporal Evidence (2/2) +
  Independent Verification (1/2) +
  Policy Application Proof (0/1) +
  Contextual Constraints (1/1)

Result:

4 / 6 requirements met → Decision NOT YET permissible

This immediately answers:
	•	What’s missing
	•	How much is missing
	•	What to do next
	•	When you’re done

How this preserves trust (important)

This does not mean the system is pretending certainty.

Instead, it’s saying:

“Given the type of decision you’re trying to make, these are the minimum evidentiary conditions required for us to allow it.”

You are not claiming truth.
You are claiming procedural permission.

That’s defensible.

⸻

How the “equation” should behave (rules)

To avoid this becoming arbitrary, the system must:
	1.	Declare requirements up front
(Not invent them dynamically)
	2.	Count only admissible evidence
(Documents must qualify, not just exist)
	3.	Allow OR conditions
(“1 interview OR 1 independent document”)
	4.	Explain why each requirement exists (optional, expandable)
	5.	Never move the goalposts mid-case
(Requirements freeze once review begins)

You already have most of this logic implicitly.
This just surfaces it.
