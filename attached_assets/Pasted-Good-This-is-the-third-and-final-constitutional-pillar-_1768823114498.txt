Measurement Envelope Protocol (v1.0)

Status

This document constitutes constitutional law governing all measurement, scoring, indexing, and quantitative outputs within ELI.

It is authoritative, versioned, and immutable once ratified.

⸻

1. Purpose

The Measurement Envelope Protocol exists to prevent metric absolutism, authority laundering, and retroactive justification by binding every measurement to the epistemic conditions under which it was produced.

No measurement may exist without an explicit envelope defining:
	•	what it can support,
	•	what it cannot support,
	•	and under which epistemic constraints it was generated.

⸻

2. Core Principle

Metrics do not speak for themselves.
Envelopes speak for metrics.

A measurement without an envelope is epistemically invalid.

⸻

3. Definitions

3.1 Measurement

Any scalar, vector, index, score, category, or quantitative output produced by the system, including but not limited to:
	•	Epistemic Load Index (ELI),
	•	sub-scores,
	•	normalized values,
	•	thresholds,
	•	or comparisons.

3.2 Measurement Envelope

A structured, immutable record describing the epistemic conditions under which a measurement was produced.

The envelope is authoritative; the numeric value is subordinate.

⸻

4. Envelope Requirements (Mandatory)

Every measurement must be accompanied by a Measurement Envelope containing:
	•	measurement_id
	•	measurement_type
	•	ruleset_version
	•	decision_time_anchor (DTA)
	•	locked_strata_referenced[]
	•	admissible_record_hash
	•	excluded_information_classes[]
	•	dominant_constraints[]
	•	epistemic_volume_descriptor
	•	authorized_uses[]
	•	prohibited_uses[]

Measurements lacking a complete envelope are invalid.

⸻

5. Stratification Compliance

AXIOM M1 — Outcome Blindness

Measurements must be computed using only locked S1 and S2 strata.

Outcome knowledge (S3) and narrative strata (S4) are categorically excluded.

⸻

AXIOM M2 — Non-Upward Authority

Measurements may not be used to:
	•	infer individual competence,
	•	assign blame,
	•	justify discipline,
	•	or override admissibility limits.

Any attempt to do so mandates refusal.

⸻

AXIOM M3 — Envelope Precedes Interpretation

Interpretation without envelope inspection is invalid.

Systems must refuse to display, compare, or reason over measurements if the envelope is not explicitly acknowledged.

⸻

6. Authorized vs Prohibited Uses

6.1 Authorized Uses (Exhaustive)

Measurements may be used to:
	•	constrain downstream review posture,
	•	calibrate permissible certainty,
	•	trigger system-level learning pathways,
	•	compare constraint environments, not actors.

⸻

6.2 Prohibited Uses (Categorical)

Measurements may never be used to:
	•	rank individuals,
	•	evaluate performance,
	•	predict future behavior,
	•	justify sanctions,
	•	or serve as proxy evidence of fault.

Violation mandates refusal.

⸻

7. Comparability Constraints

AXIOM M4 — Envelope Compatibility

Two measurements may be compared only if:
	•	their envelopes share compatible constraint classes,
	•	their dominant axes align,
	•	and no prohibited use is invoked.

Scalar similarity without envelope compatibility is epistemically invalid.

⸻

8. Volumetric Context Requirement

All ELI measurements must declare an epistemic volume descriptor indicating:
	•	relative constraint density,
	•	dominant limiting axes,
	•	and degree of decision-space compression.

The volume contextualizes the score and limits interpretive latitude.

⸻

9. Prohibited Transformations

The following operations are forbidden:
	•	envelope stripping,
	•	score extraction without context,
	•	threshold redefinition post hoc,
	•	aggregation across incompatible envelopes,
	•	narrative reinterpretation of scores.

Detection mandates refusal.

⸻

10. Interface Enforcement

AXIOM M5 — Non-Bypassable Envelope Gate

No interface, export, visualization, or API may expose a measurement without its envelope.

Any attempt to separate score from envelope is a constitutional violation.
