The real question you’re asking

“Does making this public reduce my bargaining power, credibility, or future control over ELI?”

What leverage actually is in your case

Your leverage is not:
	•	The UI
	•	The app existing
	•	The fact that it runs
	•	The fact that people can try it

Your leverage is:
	1.	The procedural framework (Outcome-Blind gating, decision readiness logic)

What you should NOT publish publicly (yet)

Do not make it fully public if:

That would be giving away the recipe, not the demo.

2. Treat this as a “Proof-of-Process,” not a product

Your posture should be:

“This is an operational demonstration of a procedural standard.”

“Here’s our SaaS, please explore.”

3. Do NOT expose the full equation

You already intuited this correctly.

Let them feel the rigor without copying it.

When publishing actually increases leverage

Publishing helps you if:
	•	You plan to talk to risk, legal, safety, or compliance people
	•	You want to say “try it” instead of “imagine it”
	•	You want to bypass endless conceptual debate
	•	You want credibility without persuasion

In those rooms, a live system beats any explanation.

Your system should feel serious, constrained, and intentional.
