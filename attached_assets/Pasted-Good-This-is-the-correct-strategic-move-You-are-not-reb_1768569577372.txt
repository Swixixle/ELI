Canon §0.3 — ELI Imaging (Procedural Imaging Model)

ELI Expert is formally defined as a procedural imaging system.

ELI Imaging produces structured representations of a decision’s procedural and epistemic state at a specific point in time. Its function is to determine whether a decision is in a condition that permits fair, outcome-blind evaluation by human authorities.

ELI Imaging does not render judgments, diagnoses, or determinations of correctness. It evaluates whether the conditions required for judgment are present.

Imaging Analogy (Normative)
	•	Imaging does not diagnose; it determines whether diagnosis is possible.
	•	Imaging requires acquisition protocols; incomplete scans are reported as limited.
	•	Imaging separates measurement from interpretation.
	•	Imaging explicitly discloses artifacts and limitations.
	•	Imaging outputs may be “non-diagnostic” without being erroneous.

ELI Imaging applies these same principles to governance, compliance, and risk decisions.

What ELI Imaging Captures
An ELI Imaging scan evaluates and records:
	1.	Decision Target — the specific decision under review
	2.	Temporal Boundary — the information available at decision time
	3.	Independent Verification — corroboration beyond narrative assertion
	4.	Policy Application — governing standards active at that time
	5.	Contextual Constraints — operational limits shaping available options

Failure to satisfy acquisition requirements results in a limited or unsafe scan, not a synthesized or inferred conclusion.

Output Classification (Imaging Readiness)
ELI Imaging produces readiness states, not verdicts:
	•	Advisory Only / Unsafe — evaluation procedurally inadmissible
	•	Permitted / High Risk — evaluation allowed with significant limitations
	•	Permitted / Defensible — evaluation supported by sufficient acquisition
	•	Permitted / Regulator-Ready — evaluation meets audit and regulatory standards

These outputs describe procedural visibility, not decision quality.

Separation of Roles
	•	ELI Imaging measures and records procedural structure
	•	Human authorities interpret, judge, and decide

This separation is mandatory and enforced by system architecture.
