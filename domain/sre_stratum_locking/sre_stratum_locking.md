# Stratified Recusatory Epistemology

## Stratum Locking Protocol (v1.0)

### Status

This document constitutes constitutional law governing epistemic stratification, temporal integrity, and non-retroactivity within the system.

This protocol is authoritative, versioned, and immutable once ratified.

---

## 1. Purpose

The Stratum Locking Protocol exists to prevent temporal contamination, retroactive judgment, and epistemic collapse by enforcing irreversible boundaries between epistemic layers once sufficient information has stabilized.

Stratum locking ensures that:
- decision-time knowledge remains sovereign,
- outcome knowledge cannot rewrite admissibility,
- and institutional judgment remains defensible under retrospective scrutiny.

---

## 2. Definitions

### 2.1 Epistemic Stratum

A stratum is a formally defined layer of knowledge distinguished by:
- temporal availability,
- admissible evidence types,
- permitted operations,
- and refusal conditions.

### 2.2 Canonical Strata

The system recognizes the following minimal strata:
- **S1 — Decision Substrate**: What was knowable at the moment of decision.
- **S2 — Constraint Envelope**: Time pressure, resource limits, guideline coherence, tooling, workload, and irreversibility.
- **S3 — Outcome Knowledge**: Facts revealed only after the decision.
- **S4 — Narrative Reconstruction**: Explanatory, moral, or causal stories constructed post hoc.

### 2.3 Stratum Lock

A stratum lock is a one-way, irreversible commitment that freezes the contents and admissibility rules of a stratum.

Once locked, a stratum cannot be modified, supplemented, or reinterpreted by higher strata.

---

## 3. Core Axioms

### AXIOM S1 — Unidirectional Visibility

Higher strata may observe lower strata.
Lower strata may never observe higher strata.

Any attempt at reverse visibility mandates refusal.

---

### AXIOM S2 — Non-Retroactivity

No epistemic element may be revised, augmented, or reclassified based on information that became available after the stratum's lock point.

Outcome knowledge is categorically barred from modifying decision-time strata.

---

### AXIOM S3 — Mandatory Locking

Strata must be locked before:
- evaluative judgment,
- accountability analysis,
- or institutional review occurs.

Evaluation prior to locking is invalid.

---

### AXIOM S4 — Lock Precedes Judgment

Judgment without a locked decision stratum is epistemically illegitimate.

The correct system behavior in such cases is refusal.

---

### AXIOM S5 — Irreversibility

Stratum locks are irreversible.

Unlocking, partial unlocking, or "temporary reopening" is prohibited.

---

## 4. Locking Conditions

### 4.1 S1 (Decision Substrate) Lock Trigger

S1 must be locked when:
- the decision time (DTA) is identified, and
- the admissible record for that moment is finalized.

No outcome data may be present at lock time.

---

### 4.2 S2 (Constraint Envelope) Lock Trigger

S2 must be locked immediately after S1.

Constraints must be captured as experienced, not reconstructed.

---

### 4.3 S3 (Outcome Knowledge) Lock Trigger

S3 may be populated only after S1 and S2 are locked.

S3 may never alter S1 or S2.

---

### 4.4 S4 (Narrative Reconstruction)

S4 is non-authoritative by definition.

Narratives may exist only as explanatory exhaust and are barred from evaluative authority.

---

## 5. Lock Authority

### 5.1 Authorized Locking Actors

Only designated system authorities may initiate a lock:
- Gatekeeper Substrate (automated),
- or explicitly authorized governance process.

Human discretionary overrides are prohibited.

---

### 5.2 Lock Metadata (Required)

Every lock event must produce immutable metadata:
- stratum_id
- lock_timestamp
- ruleset_version
- admissible_record_hash
- initiating_authority

Locks without metadata are invalid.

---

## 6. Prohibited Operations

The following are categorically forbidden:
- Injecting outcome data into locked strata
- Reinterpreting admissible evidence using later expertise
- Revising constraints based on hindsight
- Using narrative strata for evaluation
- "Clarifying" earlier ambiguity with later certainty

Detection of any prohibited operation mandates refusal.

---

## 7. Interaction with the Parrot Box

The Parrot Box depends on stratum locking.
- Entitlement checks must reference locked strata only.
- Any request involving unlocked strata must be refused.
- Outcome-contaminated inputs must trigger Parrot Box refusal codes.

Stratum locking is a precondition for legitimate speech.

---

## 8. Failure Handling

### 8.1 Missing Lock

If a required stratum is not locked:
- the system must refuse evaluation,
- and surface a "Stratum Not Locked" error.

### 8.2 Lock Corruption

If a lock hash mismatch is detected:
- all downstream outputs are invalidated,
- and the system must halt evaluation.

## 9. Versioning and Amendment

This protocol is frozen upon ratification.

Amendments require:
- explicit version increment,
- documented rationale,
- and re-ratification.

Backward compatibility is not assumed.

This protocol supersedes all informal epistemic practices.

No policy, convenience, or institutional pressure may override stratum locking.

Epistemic integrity requires irreversibility.
